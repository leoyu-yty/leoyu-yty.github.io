雅思写作2作文案例训练

2026/3/24 雅思 雅思 写作

此篇为雅思写作2的作文案例训练

段落结构：

1）介绍段-Introduction：改写原题观点句+自己偏向的观点（比较句）

2）先扩展不支持的一方的论点

3）On the other hand，再扩展自己支持的一方的论点

4）In conclusion，重复自己的偏向观点+理由（反驳不支持的一方的观点——简单处理）

结尾段：

In conclusion, I strongly believe that …重复自己偏向的观点+理由… Although…引出对方的理由…，… 反驳这个理由…

原因参考：

钱 / 身体健康 / 心情 / 压力 / 学习表现 / 工作表现 / 就业机会 / 安全 / 犯罪 / 环境保护 / 培养or缺乏什么能力 / 培养好or坏性格 / 生活质量提高or下降

一个原因段论证：

开门见山提出论点——because原因分析 which——for example具体论证分析——In contrast / if 反证法——In conclusion改写重复第一个句子

社会现象题型：

开头：改写原题现象 + 在我看来，有两个原因导致此现象+影响是好的/坏的

Discuss题型：some people think that A观点, while others believe that B观点

案例一：

题目：

Some university students want to learn about other subjects in addition to their main subjects. Others believe it is more important to give all their time and attention to studying for a qualification.
Discuss both these views and give your own opinion.

作文内容：
1、Introduction

In college, some students take various courses apart from their main subjects, while others focus entirely on a qualification.

While learning different subjects has some benefits, I believe that concentrating on one key qualification is generally more effective.

2、核心段1（分）

Studying extra subjects alongside main courses can be good for students’ career planning and job choices.

First of all, learning courses from different fields helps students find their real interests.

When they spend more time studying the subjects they like, they can plan their future career path earlier and prepare for it in advance.

Besides, students with wider knowledge have more employment chances, because they develop skills in different areas and build a solid foundation, which makes them more competitive in the job market.

3、核心段2（一段）

On the other hand, focusing fully on a specific qualification is better for students’ academic results and future career development.

Trying to study too many subjects at the same time can lower students’ grades. Main major courses usually include exams, essays and plenty of homework, which need a lot of time and effort. Good grades in major subjects are also very important for postgraduate study applications and job interviews, as they show professional ability. If students take too many extra courses, their attention will be divided and they will have less time for main courses, which will harm their future career development.

4、总结

In conclusion, I strongly believe that focusing on one specific qualification brings more benefits to college students’ academic performance and future prospects.

Even though learning different subjects can help students develop comprehensive skills, it can distract their attention and lead to poorer academic results in their main field.

案例二（经典）

题目：

It is important for people to take risks, both in their professional lives and their personal lives.

Do you think the advantages of taking risks outweigh the disadvantages?

1、介绍：

  • Some individuals tend to avoid risk in their professional and personal life, while others argue that taking risk is beneficial to one’s personal growth.

  • From my perspective, although avoiding risks can bring certain short-term benefits, the advantages of taking risks outweighs the disadvantages in the long run. （好处大于坏处outweigh，短期不好长期好）

2、核心段一：disadvantages不冒风险的好处

  • avoiding risk can bring considerable benefit to individuals.（带来好处）

  • To begin with, it contributes positively to people’s mental health.

    When people stay within their comfort zones, they don’t need to face unexpected challenges or bear heavy pressure, which reduce the likelihood of anxiety and other psychological problems.

  • In addition, employees who prefer stability are often favored by employers. Companies generally value reliable and steady staff members, as they can maintain consistent performance and ensure smooth operation of the workplace.

3、核心段二：advantages冒风险的好处

  • However, the benefits of taking risks are far more significant. （坏处更多）

  • Firstly, taking risks provides people with more opportunities.

    It is widely acknowledged that risks and opportunities coexist. For instant, high-risk investments such as technology stocks usually have greater growth potential, enabling investors to gain considerable profits.

  • Furthermore, facing risks helps individuals develop strong personal qualities.

    People who take risks often experience high pressure and uncertainty, but these difficulties can inspire their potential and make them more resilient and confident, which is beneficial for their career development.

    A typical example is Elon Musk, whose rocket recycling project was once doubted by the public. Despite the huge risks, he persisted and finally achieved remarkable success.

4、结论：

  • In conclusion, I strongly believe that taking risks can offer more valuable rewards, including more opportunity for success and the cultivation of strong personal qualities.

  • Although avoiding risk can help people stay in a comfortable zone, protect their mental health and ensure work performance, these benefits are only short-term and cannot help individuals achieve long-term growth or realize their full potential.

案例三：

题目：

In many countries nowadays, consumers can go to a supermarket and buy food produced all over the world. Do you think this is a positive or negative development?

1、开头：
  • In modern society, people can easily buy food from all over the world in local supermarkets.
  • While some people argue that this trend is a negative development, I firmly believe that its benefits far outweigh the drawbacks.
2、核心段一：

  • Some people argue that buying food from across the globe in supermarket is a negative development.

  • Firstly, imported food can pose potential risk to public health.

    To keep food fresh during long-distance transportation, food factories often add plenty of chemical medicines to these products, which can cause health problems.

  • Besides, the excessive introduction of imported food products can do harm to local farmers and local economy.

    Local farmers grow food in their own country, but they cannot compete with cheap imported food from big foreign companies.

    As a result, many farmers will lose their jobs and agriculture economy will be effected negatively.

3、核心段二：

  • While others believe that this trend brings much benefits and has a positive effect on our daily life.

  • First of all, people can have more food choices and have a better diet.

    In the past people can only eat food grew in their own countries and in season. Now, they can buy all kinds of food from various countries, such as tropical mangoes in cold European countries, which not only improves people’s happiness but also help them maintain a balanced and nutritious diet.

  • What’s more, the global food trade can boost economic growth and create job opportunities in both exporting and importing countries.

    Exporting countries can expand their agricultural industries, which helps local farmers live a better life.

    At the same time, importing counties can create more jobs in supermarkets, logistics and food processing.

  • Additionally, global food supply chain can help stabilize food prices and reduce the risk of food shortage caused by regional natural disasters.

4、结尾段：
  • In conclusion, although buying global food in supermarkets has some downsides, including potential health risks and negative impact on local agriculture, I still hold the view that its advantages are more significant, as it can rich public food choices, improving life qualities and boost economic growth worldwide.

案例四：

题目：有人认为生活在大城市对人们的健康有害，你是否赞成此观点
1、介绍：

  • An increasing number of people choose to settle in big cities to pursue better life opportunities.

    However, there is a widespread debate about whether living in big cities is harmful to people’s health.

  • Although big cities offer better living and medical conditions, the negative impacts they bring to people’s physical and mental health outweigh the benefits.

2、核心段一

  • It’s undeniable that living in big cities has certain advantages for people’s health.

  • On one hand, big cities are equipped with more comprehensive infrastructures and a higher standard of living.

    There are numerous sports facilities like gyms, stadiums and public fitness areas, as well as various leisure and entertainment places including parks, cinemas and cultural centers. These places provide people with convenient ways to relax themselves and maintain physical activities, which is beneficial to both physical and mental health.

  • On the other hands, big cities have advanced medical resources and convenient medical services.

    Top-level hospitals, professional medical teams and advanced medical equipment are concentrated in big cities, enabling residents to receive timely and high-quality medical care when they are ill, which helps to reduce the risk of serious health problems.

3、核心段二

  • Despite the above benefits, living in big cities has more negative effects on people’s health.

  • Firstly, air pollution in big cities is more serious.

    With a large number of factories and vehicles, harmful gases are constantly emitted into the air, while the area of vegetation, which can purify the air, is relatively small. Long-term exposure to polluted air can cause respiratory diseases.(呼吸道疾病)

  • Secondly, noise pollution is also a severe problem in big cities.

    The constant noise from vehicles, construction sites and crowds can disturb people’s rest, affect their mood and even lead to mental anxiety in the long run.

  • Thirdly, the high cost of living in big cities forces many people to choose cheap fast food, which are usually high in fat, salt and sugar but lack essential vitamins and nutrients. This unhealthy diet can easily lead to health issues like obesity.

  • Finally, people in big cities are faced with enormous work and life pressure.

    The fierce competition often make people have irregular rest schedules, resulting in lack of sleep, which may trigger potential health problems such as heart diseases.

4、总结
  • In conclusion, while living in big cities can bring convenience in terms of (就…而言) living conditions and medical care, its harms to people’s health are much more obvious, including serious air and noise pollution, unhealthy diet and irregular work and rest caused by high pressure and living cost.
  • Therefore, I agree with the view that living in big cities is harmful to people’s health.

案例五

题目：很多大学的学生没有足够多的学生选择科学专业
1、介绍

  • Nowadays, a growing number of students are reluctant to choose science majors, which has become a noticeable trend in many colleges.

    From my perspective, there are three main reasons behind this phenomenon, and its impact on social development and scientific progress is negative.

2、核心段一

  • Science subjects are widely perceived as difficult and demanding.

    They involve a large number of mathematical formulas and cutting-edge research findings, which pose considerable challenges to students. Moreover, learners have to devote a huge amount of time to conducting experiments and scientific research in laboratories.

    As a result, many students are discouraged from choosing science and tend to opt for easier academic disciplines instead.

3、核心段二

  • Graduates majoring in science may find it hard to achieve rapid career success in the short term.

    They need to spend years conducting persistent research, accumulating professional experience and making innovations before gaining recognition in their fields, which discourages students who pursue quick career advancement.

4、核心段三

  • Many students and parents hold the belief that science-related jobs are less stable and profitable compared with professions like business, law or finance.

    Consequently, they are more likely to choose subjects that promise higher salaries, rather than science programs.

5、结论
  • In conclusion, the lack of interests among university students for science majors is mainly attributed to the difficulty of science subjects and the relatively slow career development and less attractive income prospects of science-related jobs.

案例六

题目：In many countries nowadays, consumers can go to a supermarket and buy food produced all over the world. Do you think this is a positive or negative development?
1、介绍

  • In contemporary society, a growing number of supermarkets enable consumers to purchase food products sourced from across the globe.

    While I acknowledge that this trend may pose threats to（用于替换have a negative impact on） public health and local agriculture, I firmly believe that its overall benefits far outweigh the drawbacks.

2、核心段一

  • On the negative side, imported food can undermine local agricultural industries and present health risks.

  • First, cheap foreign food takes away business from local farmers.

    As more people choose imported food instead of local produce, local farmers will earn less money, and many may lose their jobs.

  • Secondly, to extend the shelf life of foods during long-distance transportation, companies often add chemicals.

    Excessive consumption of these chemicals can have adverse(代替negative) effects on consumers’ well-being, especially on children.

3、核心段二

  • However, the advantages of global food supply are substantial(大量的).

  • Primarily, it grants consumers access to a much broader range of dietary options.

    Instead of being limited by seasonal or geographical food products, people can enjoy fresh produce year-round.

    For example, individuals in temperate regions like the UK can readily purchase tropical fruits such as mangoes and avocados, while those in coastal areas have access to a diverse selection of seafood. This variety not only enhances dining experiences but also ensures a more balanced nutrient intake.

  • Furthermore, the international food supply chain generates significant economic value.

    It creates numerous job opportunities in logistics, warehousing, and retail, which are often more numerous than the jobs lost in local competing industries. For exporting nations, this trade also stimulates agricultural development and boosts national economies.

4、总结

  • In conclusion, I strongly believe that the import of food from other countries brings far more benefits to people’s daily lives and helps boost the local economy.

    Although it may cause some short-term negative effects, such as harming local agriculture and bringing potential health risks, these problems can be solved by making stricter food safety rules and using more advanced food preservation technologies.

案例七

题目：有些人认为选择自己喜欢的工作更重要，有些人认为基于现实找工作更好
1、开头

  • When it comes to choosing a job, people hold different views.

    Some people prefer to do work they love, while others believe that finding a practical job based on real life is more important.

  • Although a job that brings personal interest can be fulfilling, I firmly believe that prioritizing practicality when seeking employment is a better choice for long-term stability and success.

2、核心段一

  • On one hand, there are clear benefits to choosing a job based on personal interest.

  • Firstly, doing work we enjoy can improve our mood and keep us motivated.

    This helps us work harder and stay positive, even when facing challenges at work.

  • Additionally, passion for a job can inspire creativity.

    When we love what we do, we are more likely to think of new ideas and improve our skills, which can help our career develop in the long run.

    For example, many talented writers and artists succeed because they are truly passionate about their work, such as J.K Rowling.

  • Therefore, choosing a favorite job can bring us long-term motivation and creative growth, making work a source of personal satisfaction.

3、核心段二

  • On the other hand, I believe that choosing a practical job is more reasonable for most people.

  • First of all, practical jobs usually offer higher salaries and more stability. (与就业有关一定要提这两点)

    This allows us to live a comfortable life without worrying about money problems.

  • Moreover, practical jobs are often a good match for our existing abilities. （与自身能力相匹配）

    This means we can perform better than other job seekers and have more chances to get employed.

    For instance, a graduate who majored in accounting can easily find a stable job in a finance company, which not only offers a steady monthly income but also clear promotion paths, helping them build a secure career。

    Overall, a practical job not only meets our basic living needs but also gives us a strong competitive edge in the job market, laying a solid foundation for our future.

4、总结

  • In conclusion, I strongly believe that choosing a practical job is the superior option.

    It provides financial security and stability, which are essential for a peaceful life and reliable career development.

  • Although a job we love may bring personal satisfaction, it often lacks economic security and long-term stability, which may bring pressure and uncertainty to our life.

案例八

题目：Some people have decided to reduce the number of times they fly every year or to stop flying altogether. Do you think the environmental benefits of this development outweigh the disadvantages for individuals and businesses?
1、开头（改写+Although）
  • Some individuals are choosing to reduce their annual air travel or give up flying completely.
  • Although cutting back on flights brings clear environmental benefits, I believe the disadvantages this trend brings to individuals and businesses are far more significant.
2、核心段一

  • Admittedly, reducing air travel can bring much environmental benefits.

  • First, airplanes rely heavily on non-renewable fossil fuels, and avoiding on flights directly reduce fuel consumptions.

    Excessive extraction of oil, which is the primary fuel of planes, can cause severe damage to ecosystems, such as deforestation destruction of wildlife habitats and water pollution.

  • Additionally, fewer flights means less air pollution from aircraft emissions and less noise pollution around the airports, which improve the quality of life for local residents.

  • Furthermore, this trend encourages people to adapt more eco-friendly travel alternatives, such as railways and trains, which have a much lower carbon footprint.

3、核心段二

  • However, the disadvantages of reducing air travel for individuals and businesses are far more impactful.

  • For individuals, replacing air travel with other transport means significantly linger journey times.

    This not only leads to potential delays for work but also consumes a great deal of work and energy, which may reduce work efficiency.

  • Moreover, many remote destinations or overseas countries are inaccessible without flights, which disrupts people’s travel plans and makes it difficult to meet with family members living aboard.

  • For business, especially for those relying on international trade, fewer flights reduce face-to-face meetings with clients and partners, which may damage business relationships and reduce economic opportunities.

4、总结

  • In conclusion, I strongly believe that the disadvantages of reducing air travel for individuals and businesses far outweigh its environmental benefits.

    This is because cutting back on flights severely disrupts people’s daily lives, work efficiency and the development of global businesses.

    Although reducing flights can help cut carbon emissions and protect the environment, these ecological gains are not enough to offset the huge practical losses that individuals and enterprises suffer.(背一下)

案例九（原因+解决方案）（典型）

题目：In some countries, more and more people are becoming interested in finding out about the history of the house or building they live in.

What are the reasons for this?
How can people research this?

1、开头（改写+引出回答）
  • In recent years, an increasing number of people in some countries have developed a strong interest in exploring the history of the houses or buildings they reside in. （改写原文）
  • This phenomenon is driven by several factors, and there are various practical ways for people to conduct such research.（背——原因+解决方案题型固定开头句式）
2、核心段一

  • The growing interest in the history of one’s own house can be mainly attributed to two key reasons.（有几个原因）

  • First, the number of people who have received formal history education has been on the rise. （人数上升）

    At present, history courses have become a compulsory subject in many primary and secondary schools. From an early age, people begin to learn about the history of their own country and the city where they live, which helps them build a solid foundation of historical knowledge.

    When they eventually purchase their own houses, it is natural for them to extend this interest to the specific historical stories of the area where their houses are located.

  • Secondly, the influence of the internet plays a crucial role.

    In recent years, historical films and short videos have gained growing popularity, attracting more and more people with their engaging themes. （短视频的影响）

    This rising enthusiasm for historical content has inspired people to find out about the history of their own places of residence.

3、核心段二

  • There are multiple effective methods for people to research the history of their houses.（解决方法段开头）

  • To begin with, they can look for relevant historical materials.

    By visiting local libraries, people can borrow books related to the local history, which enables them to understand the historical background of the area where they live.

  • In addition, searching online is a convenient and efficient way.

    With the widespread use of the internet, a large amount of historical information about local buildings and communities is available on various websites, allowing people to access relevant content anytime and anywhere.

  • Moreover, visiting museums is another valuable approach. （博物馆）

    Compared with theoretical research, museums display more tangible historical records, such as old houses, traditional cultural relics and related tools. These exhibits help people visualize the scenes of the past more vividly.

  • Finally, consulting the elderly in the neighborhood is also a practical way.

    The elderly can share the historical scenes they have witnessed from their own perspectives, as well as stories about their ancestors who lived in this area, providing a more personal and vivid insight into the local history.

4、总结（回答两个问题）
  • In conclusion, the rising interest in the history of one’s own house stems from the popularization of history education and the influence of historical media.
  • Meanwhile, people can adopt diverse methods such as consulting libraries, searching online, visiting museums and talking to the elderly to explore the historical stories behind their houses.

案例十

题目：It is important for everyone, including young people（侧重点）, to save money for their future. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this statement?
1、开头
  • Nowadays, people of all ages are advised to save money for the future.
  • While saving has certain benefits, I believe its disadvantages are more obvious, especially for young people.
2、核心段一
  • Admittedly, saving money is beneficial in two main ways.
  • It acts as a reliable financial buffer for unexpected situations, such as sudden health issues that demand high medical expenses, which can easily put people in a difficult financial position without savings. Having a certain amount of savings helps people avoid the embarrassment of being unable to afford treatment or having to ask others for help.
  • Additionally, it cultivates rational spending habits, as the practice of saving encourages people to think twice before making impulsive purchases—such as buying unnecessary clothes or gadgets—and thus reduces unnecessary regrets, helping them manage their personal finances more wisely.
3、核心段二
  • However, excessive saving brings more problems, especially for young people.
  • Inflation gradually erodes the value of saved money; the purchasing power of money saved today will decrease over time, making it far less beneficial than reasonable investment.
  • Moreover, it deprives young people of valuable chances to enjoy life or invest in themselves, such as traveling to broaden their horizons, learning new professional skills, or even trying small entrepreneurial projects.
  • Furthermore, the pressure of over-saving may harm their physical and mental health, such as relying on cheap, unhealthy fast food to cut costs or overworking to save more, which can lead to fatigue and anxiety.
4、总结

  • In conclusion, though saving helps with emergencies and rational spending,

    prioritizing reasonable spending and investment over excessive saving is better for everyone, especially young people, as it avoids money devaluation and offers more growth opportunities.

案例十一（原因+positive / negative）

题目：In their advertising, businesses nowadays usually emphasize that their products are new in some way.

Why is this? Do you think it is a positive or negative development?

1、开头
  • Companies usually highlight innovations in their new products in advertisements.
  • This phenomenon is driven by several key factors, and I believe it has an positive impact on both the market and consumers.（背）
2、核心段一

  • The growing trend of businesses emphasizing product novelty in advertising can be mainly attributed to two main reasons.（背）

  • For businesses, highlighting innovation allows them to charge a higher price for their products.

    A new function means the latest version is more practical than its previous versions, enabling companies to set a higher price and earn more profits.

    Besides, in a competitive market, emphasizing novelty helps businesses stand out from competitors and attract more customers.

  • For consumers, this focus on new features also meets their daily needs.

    Modern customers want products that can make their life more convenient, and by advertising innovations, companies help consumers find products that fit their needs quickly.

3、核心段二

  • Also, I believe the advantages of this trend far outweigh the disadvantages.（背）

  • First, this practice strongly encourages innovation and promotes technological development.

    Companies are motivated to develop new features to beat competitors in the same industry, which speeds up technological progress in many fields. For example, smartphone brands keep advertising new functions, pushing the whole industry to improve.

  • Besides, this competition also creates more job opportunities, especially for new graduates.

    Students learn the latest technology at university, and companies need these skills to develop new products, so they are willing to hire graduates, which helps reduce the unemployment rate.

4、总结

  • In conclusion, businesses emphasize product novelty in advertising to gain higher profits and meet consumer demand.

    This trend is positive, as it encourages innovation, creates jobs, and brings better products to people.

案例十二

题目：Caring for children is probably the most important thing of the society. It is suggested that all mothers and fathers should be required to take childcare training courses. To what extent do you agree or disagree with the statement?
1、开头
  • Nowadays, parenting has become increasingly important in modern society.
  • Although some people argue that it is unnecessary for parents to take compulsory childcare courses, I believe the benefits of such training far outweigh the drawbacks, especially for young parents.
2、核心段一

  • Admittedly, allowing parents greater freedom in raising their children has certain merits.

  • Firstly, families can save a considerable amount of money.

    Attending professional training courses usually involves significant fees（花大钱）, which parents could instead spend on more practical items such as high-quality baby formula to provide better nutrition for their children.

  • In addition, children may develop positive personalities such as independence with less strict parental control.

    Without being restricted by excessive rules, children are more likely to solve problems on their own, become braver, and maintain curiosity about new things, thus improving their self-reliance.（thus递进）

3、核心段二

  • Nevertheless, attending childcare training courses is far more beneficial.

  • To begin with, such courses equip parents, especially young ones, with more practical experience in childcare.

    Young couples often lack experience in looking after infants, and training programs can effectively meet their needs and help them handle unexpected situations.（处理意料之外的情况）

  • Furthermore, children’s physical and mental health can be greatly improved.

    Childcare courses usually stress the importance of a balanced diet and regular outdoor exercise. With this knowledge, parents are more willing to prepare nutritious meals and increase their children’s physical activities, which reduces the risk of childhood obesity and promotes greater well-being.

4、总结

  • In conclusion, although giving parents the freedom to raise children can save money and help children develop qualities such as courage and independence,

    I firmly believe that requiring parents to take childcare training is necessary. It enriches parents’ practical skills and boosts the overall physical and mental health of children.

案例十三

题目：
1、开头
  • Nowadays, an increasing number of parents choose to educate their children at home instead of sending them to traditional schools. While this approach offers certain benefits in specific scenarios, I firmly believe that its drawbacks far outweigh its advantages.
2、核心段一

  • Admittedly, educating children at home has several merits that contribute to children’s short-term growth and family convenience.

  • To begin with, the one-on-one teaching mode enables parents to tailor the learning content and pace to their children’s individual needs. Unlike schools where teachers have to cater to the average level of the whole class, parents can identify their children’s strengths and weaknesses more accurately—for example, spending more time helping a child who struggles with math while accelerating the progress for a child who excels in language. This targeted guidance can effectively improve children’s learning efficiency and help them master knowledge more solidly.

  • Besides, home education can reduce both time and financial expenditures for families. Parents no longer need to spend money on school fees, textbooks, uniforms, or transportation costs associated with sending children to school. Meanwhile, the time spent on commuting to and from school can be saved, allowing children to have more flexible time for extracurricular learning, hobbies, or family bonding.

  • A third minor advantage is that home education can provide a safer and more comfortable learning environment. In some cases, children may face bullying, peer pressure, or negative influences from bad classmates at school, which can affect their learning mood and mental health. Home education avoids such risks, allowing children to learn in a familiar and stress-free atmosphere.

3、核心段二

  • Despite the above benefits, home education exerts more negative impacts on children’s long-term development, especially in terms of their personality cultivation, social skills, and academic comprehensiveness.

  • Firstly, children educated at home are likely to become introverted and even suffer from mental health issues. Since they lack regular interactions with peers of the same age, they have few opportunities to express their opinions, share emotions, or resolve conflicts with others. Over time, this isolation may lead to feelings of loneliness, anxiety, or low self-esteem, and in severe cases, it may even develop into hidden mental disorders that affect their future life.

  • What’s more, keeping children away from group learning environments deprives them of the chance to develop essential social skills and a sense of responsibility. In schools, children learn to communicate, cooperate, and compromise with their classmates through group activities, team projects, and daily interactions. They also learn to abide by rules, take on responsibilities (such as being a class monitor or group leader), and understand the importance of teamwork—skills that are crucial for their future careers and social life. Home-educated children, however, miss out on these valuable learning experiences, which may make it difficult for them to adapt to social life when they grow up.

  • Another significant disadvantage is that most parents lack professional teaching qualifications and resources, which may lead to incomplete or unbalanced academic education. Schools employ teachers who have received professional training in education theories, teaching methods, and subject knowledge, ensuring that children receive systematic and comprehensive education. In contrast, many parents may not be proficient in all subjects, and they may not know how to design scientific teaching plans or assess their children’s academic progress. This can result in gaps in children’s knowledge and affect their future academic development, especially when they need to enter higher education or the workplace.

4、总结
  • In conclusion, although home education can bring benefits such as improved learning efficiency, reduced time and financial costs, and a safer learning environment, its drawbacks are more significant. It not only hinders the development of children’s social skills and personality but also may lead to unbalanced academic education and mental health problems. Therefore, sending children to school is still a more reasonable choice for their long-term development.

案例十四

题目：

Some people claim many things that children are taught at school are a waste of time. Other people argue that everything taught at school is useful at some time. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

1、开头
  • Some people claim that some courses and content taught at school are meaningless to children, while some people hold the opposite opinion. Although school education contains some meaningless elements in certain aspects, I firmly believe that the knowledge and skills taught at school are highly practical for students’ future development.
2、核心段一
  • Admittedly, school education is meaningless in several aspects.
  • Firstly, many people’s career paths are completely unrelated to what they have learnt at school. For instance, subjects like mathematics have little relationship with most people’s jobs, while every student approximately spends a decade or more from kindergarten to college focusing on it. Considering realistic factors, learning such knowledge is both time-consuming and meaningless for pursuing better career development in the future.
  • Besides, many schools emphasize examination grades and students’ academic performance rather than practical skills and the understanding of knowledge. Students are compelled to focus on their academic grades, leading to the loss of their practical life skills such as communication, financial management and time management, which play a more significant role in helping them adapt to society.
3、核心段二

  • Despite the above drawbacks, the benefits brought by school education are far more significant and profound in various fields.

  • To begin with, the subjects taught at school are the basic knowledge in many fields. Even if these subjects like mathematics and literature are less practical than science and programming skills, they can boost critical thinking and creativity, which are beneficial for students’ career development. These underlying abilities can help young learners solve problems more effectively and adapt to new challenges in their future work.

  • In addition, the various basic subjects taught at school can encourage students to explore their interests in specific areas. Through finding the field they are interested in at a young age, students can choose their majors and career paths in advance. Meanwhile, they will have more time and energy to deepen their knowledge in one field, improving their expertise and helping them stand out from other candidates in the future job market.

4、总结
  • In conclusion, although school education teaches students some seemingly meaningless subjects which are not practical enough and time-consuming, I firmly believe that its positive impacts far outweigh the negative ones, including developing students’ interests in specific fields in advance and building a solid foundation for their further career development. Therefore, I strongly support the idea that school education is useful at some point in their lives.
