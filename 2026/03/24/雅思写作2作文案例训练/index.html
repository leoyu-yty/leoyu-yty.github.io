雅思写作2作文案例训练

2026/3/24 雅思 雅思 写作

此篇为雅思写作2的作文案例训练

段落结构：

1）介绍段-Introduction：改写原题观点句+自己偏向的观点（比较句）

2）先扩展不支持的一方的论点

3）On the other hand，再扩展自己支持的一方的论点

4）In conclusion，重复自己的偏向观点+理由（反驳不支持的一方的观点——简单处理）

结尾段：

In conclusion, I strongly believe that …重复自己偏向的观点+理由… Although…引出对方的理由…，… 反驳这个理由…

题型：

1）社会现象

开头：改写原题现象 + 在我看来，有两个原因导致此现象+影响是好的/坏的

案例一：

题目：

Some university students want to learn about other subjects in addition to their main subjects. Others believe it is more important to give all their time and attention to studying for a qualification.
Discuss both these views and give your own opinion.

作文内容：
1、Introduction

In college, some students take various courses apart from their main subjects, while others focus entirely on a qualification.

While learning different subjects has some benefits, I believe that concentrating on one key qualification is generally more effective.

2、核心段1（分）

Studying extra subjects alongside main courses can be good for students’ career planning and job choices.

First of all, learning courses from different fields helps students find their real interests.

When they spend more time studying the subjects they like, they can plan their future career path earlier and prepare for it in advance.

Besides, students with wider knowledge have more employment chances, because they develop skills in different areas and build a solid foundation, which makes them more competitive in the job market.

3、核心段2（一段）

On the other hand, focusing fully on a specific qualification is better for students’ academic results and future career development.

Trying to study too many subjects at the same time can lower students’ grades. Main major courses usually include exams, essays and plenty of homework, which need a lot of time and effort. Good grades in major subjects are also very important for postgraduate study applications and job interviews, as they show professional ability. If students take too many extra courses, their attention will be divided and they will have less time for main courses, which will harm their future career development.

4、总结

In conclusion, I strongly believe that focusing on one specific qualification brings more benefits to college students’ academic performance and future prospects.

Even though learning different subjects can help students develop comprehensive skills, it can distract their attention and lead to poorer academic results in their main field.

案例二：

题目：

It is important for people to take risks, both in their professional lives and their personal lives.

Do you think the advantages of taking risks outweigh the disadvantages?

1、介绍：

  • Some individuals tend to avoid risk in their professional and personal life, while others argue that taking risk is beneficial to one’s personal growth.

  • From my perspective, although avoiding risks can bring certain short-term benefits, the advantages of taking risks outweighs the disadvantages in the long run.

2、核心段一：disadvantages不冒风险的好处

  • avoiding risk can bring considerable benefit to individuals.

  • To begin with, it contributes positively to people’s mental health.

    When people stay within their comfort zones, they don’t need to face unexpected challenges or bear heavy pressure, which reduce the likelihood of anxiety and other psychological problems.

  • In addition, employees who prefer stability are often favored by employers. Companies generally value reliable and steady staff members, as they can maintain consistent performance and ensure smooth operation of the workplace.

3、核心段二：advantages冒风险的好处

  • However, the benefits of taking risks are far more significant.

  • Firstly, taking risks provides people with more opportunities.

    It is widely acknowledged that risks and opportunities coexist. For instant, high-risk investments such as technology stocks usually have greater growth potential, enabling investors to gain considerable profits.

  • Furthermore, facing risks helps individuals develop strong personal qualities.

    People who take risks often experience high pressure and uncertainty, but these difficulties can inspire their potential and make them more resilient and confident, which is beneficial for their career development.

    A typical example is Elon Musk, whose rocket recycling project was once doubted by the public. Despite the huge risks, he persisted and finally achieved remarkable success.

4、结论：

  • In conclusion, I strongly believe that taking risks can offer more valuable rewards, including more opportunity for success and the cultivation of strong personal qualities.

  • Although avoiding risk can help people stay in a comfortable zone, protect their mental health and ensure work performance, these benefits are only short-term and cannot help individuals achieve long-term growth or realize their full potential.

案例三：

题目：

In many countries nowadays, consumers can go to a supermarket and buy food produced all over the world. Do you think this is a positive or negative development?

1、开头：
  • In modern society, people can easily buy food from all over the world in local supermarkets.
  • While some people argue that this trend is a negative development, I firmly believe that its benefits far outweigh the drawbacks.
2、核心段一：

  • Some people argue that buying food from across the globe in supermarket is a negative development.

  • Firstly, imported food can pose potential risk to public health.

    To keep food fresh during long-distance transportation, food factories often add plenty of chemical medicines to these products, which can cause health problems.

  • Besides, the excessive introduction of imported food products can do harm to local farmers and local economy.

    Local farmers grow food in their own country, but they cannot compete with cheap imported food from big foreign companies.

    As a result, many farmers will lose their jobs and agriculture economy will be effected negatively.

3、核心段二：

  • While others believe that this trend brings much benefits and has a positive effect on our daily life.

  • First of all, people can have more food choices and have a better diet.

    In the past people can only eat food grew in their own countries and in season. Now, they can buy all kinds of food from various countries, such as tropical mangoes in cold European countries, which not only improves people’s happiness but also help them maintain a balanced and nutritious diet.

  • What’s more, the global food trade can boost economic growth and create job opportunities in both exporting and importing countries.

    Exporting countries can expand their agricultural industries, which helps local farmers live a better life.

    At the same time, importing counties can create more jobs in supermarkets, logistics and food processing.

  • Additionally, global food supply chain can help stabilize food prices and reduce the risk of food shortage caused by regional natural disasters.

4、结尾段：
  • In conclusion, although buying global food in supermarkets has some downsides, including potential health risks and negative impact on local agriculture, I still hold the view that its advantages are more significant, as it can rich public food choices, improving life qualities and boost economic growth worldwide.

案例四：

题目：有人认为生活在大城市对人们的健康有害，你是否赞成此观点
1、介绍：

  • An increasing number of people choose to settle in big cities to pursue better life opportunities.

    However, there is a widespread debate about whether living in big cities is harmful to people’s health.

  • Although big cities offer better living and medical conditions, the negative impacts they bring to people’s physical and mental health outweigh the benefits.

2、核心段一

  • It’s undeniable that living in big cities has certain advantages for people’s health.

  • On one hand, big cities are equipped with more comprehensive infrastructures and a higher standard of living.

    There are numerous sports facilities like gyms, stadiums and public fitness areas, as well as various leisure and entertainment places including parks, cinemas and cultural centers. These places provide people with convenient ways to relax themselves and maintain physical activities, which is beneficial to both physical and mental health.

  • On the other hands, big cities have advanced medical resources and convenient medical services.

    Top-level hospitals, professional medical teams and advanced medical equipment are concentrated in big cities, enabling residents to receive timely and high-quality medical care when they are ill, which helps to reduce the risk of serious health problems.

3、核心段二

  • Despite the above benefits, living in big cities has more negative effects on people’s health.

  • Firstly, air pollution in big cities is more serious.

    With a large number of factories and vehicles, harmful gases are constantly emitted into the air, while the area of vegetation, which can purify the air, is relatively small. Long-term exposure to polluted air can cause respiratory diseases.(呼吸道疾病)

  • Secondly, noise pollution is also a severe problem in big cities.

    The constant noise from vehicles, construction sites and crowds can disturb people’s rest, affect their mood and even lead to mental anxiety in the long run.

  • Thirdly, the high cost of living in big cities forces many people to choose cheap fast food, which are usually high in fat, salt and sugar but lack essential vitamins and nutrients. This unhealthy diet can easily lead to health issues like obesity.

  • Finally, people in big cities are faced with enormous work and life pressure.

    The fierce competition often make people have irregular rest schedules, resulting in lack of sleep, which may trigger potential health problems such as heart diseases.

4、总结
  • In conclusion, while living in big cities can bring convenience in terms of (就…而言) living conditions and medical care, its harms to people’s health are much more obvious, including serious air and noise pollution, unhealthy diet and irregular work and rest caused by high pressure and living cost.
  • Therefore, I agree with the view that living in big cities is harmful to people’s health.

案例五

题目：很多大学的学生没有足够多的学生选择科学专业
1、介绍

  • Nowadays, a growing number of students are reluctant to choose science majors, which has become a noticeable trend in many colleges.

    From my perspective, there are three main reasons behind this phenomenon, and its impact on social development and scientific progress is negative.

2、核心段一

  • Science subjects are widely perceived as difficult and demanding.

    They involve a large number of mathematical formulas and cutting-edge research findings, which pose considerable challenges to students. Moreover, learners have to devote a huge amount of time to conducting experiments and scientific research in laboratories.

    As a result, many students are discouraged from choosing science and tend to opt for easier academic disciplines instead.

3、核心段二

  • Graduates majoring in science may find it hard to achieve rapid career success in the short term.

    They need to spend years conducting persistent research, accumulating professional experience and making innovations before gaining recognition in their fields, which discourages students who pursue quick career advancement.

4、核心段三

  • Many students and parents hold the belief that science-related jobs are less stable and profitable compared with professions like business, law or finance.

    Consequently, they are more likely to choose subjects that promise higher salaries, rather than science programs.

5、结论
  • In conclusion, the lack of interests among university students for science majors is mainly attributed to the difficulty of science subjects and the relatively slow career development and less attractive income prospects of science-related jobs.
