雅思口语part2&part3基础训练

2026/3/29 雅思 雅思 口语

此篇为雅思口语part2&part3训练

总时长：11 -14 min

  • Part 1：4 -5 min；10 -11个问题；问题短、简单；涵盖3个主题
    • 2 -3 个问题关于你的学习，工作，住在哪
    • 4个围绕一个小话题的问题
    • 4个围绕一个小话题的问题
    • 重点注重：fluency，不要嗯嗯呐，词汇语言最简单即可
    • Part 1占分很少
  • Part 2：3 -4 min
  • Part 3：4 -5 min

考点：

  • 通顺
  • 词汇
  • 语法
  • 发音

注意：

  • 简单语言
  • 不要想太多，直接回答

一、Part 1

答题格式：Answer the question (full sentences)+ give the reason
1）2-3个简单问题
  • Do you work or are you a student
  • Do you enjoy your job / study
2）后续的话题问题
练习部分：
1、Do you take photos of buildings

  • yes, I like taking photos of buildings.

    because I think different buildings have special and beautiful designs.

2、Is there a building that you would like to visit

  • yes

    I want to visit the Forbidden City in Beijing because it is a famous ancient building in China with a long history and I missed it during my last visit to Beijing because I didn’t make a reservation.

3、Do you want to live in a tall building?

  • Yes

    Because I can enjoy a beautiful view from a tall building, and it usually makes me feel quiet and comfortable.

4、Are there tall buildings near your home?

  • Yes

    I live in the city center, so there are many modern tall buildings around my home. And I live on the 17th floor now.

5、Do you like dancing?
  • No, I’ve never dance before but I understand why other people do because it seems like fun.

二、Part 2（Describe）

1、常见话题
  • a person
    • 对象

      • family member

      • who(appearance, personality)

    • 问那些内容

      • what you did / can do there

3、话题案例
1）Describe a person who likes to look after the natural world

  • I’d like to talk about my mom, who is the person I know that loves taking care of the natural world the most.

  • First, she really likes growing plants. She has many flowers and green plants on our balcony, like roses. Different seasons have different flowers, so she often takes me to the flower market near our home to pick up new plants. It’s a great opportunity for me to see a large range of rare breeds, which is really fun.

  • Besides, she’s also a huge fan of going to the park. Every weekend, she meets her friends at the park close to our house. They sometimes take a walk around the lake, chat about their daily lives, or just sit there and enjoy a cup of coffee together, surrounded by trees and fresh air.

  • Recently, she’s picked up a new hobby: keeping fish. Last week, she bought some small tropical fish and spent almost a whole afternoon making a nice home for them, including cleaning the tank doing some decorations and adjusting the water temperature. Now she does this kind of things almost everyday.

  • I really admire my mom. She shows me how to love and take care of nature in simple, daily ways. She makes our home full of life with her plants and fish, and she also teaches me to slow down and enjoy the beauty of the natural world around us.

2）Describe a city you enjoyed visiting

  • I want to talk about Xi’an, a city I really enjoyed visiting.

  • First, Xi’an is located in central China, and it’s one of the most historic cities in the country. It was the capital of many ancient Chinese dynasties, which is why it’s packed with incredible historical sites.

  • I visited it just a few months ago, during my winter holiday, together with my college roommates. We stayed there for 9 full days, which gave us plenty of time to explore the city properly.

  • During our stay, we did a lot of fun things.

    First, we visited the Terracotta Army. It’s one of the Eight Wonders of the world, and also the tomb of Qin Shi Huang, the first emperor of China. It was so impressive to see all the clay soldiers stand up close.

    Besides, we tried lots of local food. My favorite was cold noodles, called Liangpi. It’s totally different from the noodles in other parts of China, and it was super delicious.

    We also walked around the city and saw many people wearing traditional Tang Dynasty costumes taking photos on the street. And it was a really nice view.

  • I loved this city so much for many reasons.

    First, I learned a lot about ancient China in different museums.

    Second, the local food is fantastic, and the people in Xi’an are very friendly and warm. They always helped us when we asked for directions and recommendations, which made us feel at home.

  • It was a perfect trip, and I can’t wait to pay a visit again.

3）Describe a short-term job you want to have in a foreign country
You should say: Where it is How you know of it What the job is And explain why you want to do it

  • I’d like to have a short-term job as a restaurant waiter in London, UK.

    I first learned about this kind of part-time job online, and some of my friends also recommended it to me.

    The job is basically working as a waiter in a restaurant.

    I would take orders, serve food and drinks, and help with some simple cleaning tasks.

    I really want to do this job mainly because I plan to study for my master’s degree in London in the future.

    Doing this short-term job can help me get familiar with the local environment and lifestyle in advance.

    Besides, working in a restaurant means I have to communicate with local people all the time, so it’s a perfect chance to improve my English communication skills.

    At the same time, I can earn some pocket money to cover part of my living expenses, which will reduce my financial pressure a little.

    Overall, it’s a practical and useful experience for my future study abroad.

4）didn’t enjoy the music
题目：Describe an event you attended in which you didn’t enjoy the music played
  • I want to talk about a university freshman orientation event where I think the music didn’t fit the atmosphere that it should be. It was my first year of college, and the school organized this event aiming to help new students meet and make friends. I went with two of my roommates. Initially, I thought it would be a calm, relaxed place where we could chat easily and learn about each other’s hobbies and that’s the reason why I chose to go. But when we arrived, the background music was so loud and annoying. They were playing heavy rock and upbeat pop songs, which totally contradicted with the need for quiet conversation. Everyone had to shout just to be heard, and after 10 minutes, my friends and I were all exhausted from this kind of communication style. We ended up leaving early, grabbing some other students we’d just met, and going to a quiet coffee shop nearby to actually talk and make friends properly. Looking back, I still can’t believe how bad the music choice was for that kind of event.
5）imagination
题目：Describe a time you needed to use your imagination You should say: When it was Why you needed to use imagination How difficult or easy it was And explain how you felt about it

  • I wanna talk about the birthday cake I made for my mom last year. I used my imagination during the process and the decoration.

    I didn’t want to buy a plain cake from a shop. So I spent the whole afternoon making one myself.

    I followed a tutorial online step by step. It was way harder than it looked! The cream was really tricky to handle—it kept melting. And I had to watch the oven very carefully because the baking time was super strict. If it was even a little off, the cake would get burnt. But I finally made it work.

    Then I added my own ideas. I used my favorite colors and drew little hearts and stars on it. I also put on some birthday candles and a small photo of us from when I was little.

    When she saw it, she was so happy and surprised. She said my imagination turned a simple cake into the most meaningful gift she’d ever had.

    This made me see: imagination can turn small things into something really special and full of love.

三、Part 3

1）music

  • Why do many people like listening to music while doing sports?

    • First, music gives people energy and helps them ignore physical tiredness. For example, fast - paced songs can make runners go quicker or help people lift more weights. Second, many people have formed a daily habit of listening to music. They put on headphones for almost everything, so it’s natural for them to keep this habit while doing sports too.

  • What are the differences between listening to music at home and at a live concert?

    • Well, I think the main difference is the atmosphere. At home, you have a relaxing environment with total freedom to choose music—whether it’s classical, pop, or rock.

      Live concerts are different because you share the experience with a crowd. You can’t choose the songs and there are also small rules too, like being quiet during slow songs. It’s way more social than listening alone at home.

  • What kind of music events do people like today?

    • Nowadays, live concerts are the most popular music events. First, big concerts draw huge crowds, creating an exciting, high-energy atmosphere. People can dance, sing along, and forget all the stress from work or school.

      Second, idol culture is a big reason, especially for young people. Live shows let fans meet with their favorite singers or bands up close, and I think that’s the reason why the tickets are sold out at minutes in every concert.

  • Do you think children should receive some musical education?

    • I think it’s necessary for kids to learn music at a young age. For young children, music helps with basic brain development. Singing songs with numbers or letters makes learning counting and language easier and more fun.

      For older kids, playing an instrument helps them develop an hobby in the future. Also joining a band improves teamwork with others. Soft music can also help them manage stress when school gets busy.

  • What are the differences between old and young people’s music preferences?

    • The biggest difference is the music style and energy. Older people often prefer soft, slow music like classical or traditional songs. These match their peaceful life and the music they grew up with.

      Young people, on the other hand, love high-energy, fast-paced music such as pop, rock. They deal with more daily stress from school or work, and this energetic music helps them release tension and stay motivated. Young people also follow trends, so they often listen to the newest music styles that are popular online or on social media.

  • What kind of music events are there in your country?

    • In China, we have a wide range of music events. There are traditional performances like Beijing Opera, which present our cultural heritage. On the other hand, we also host many modern live concerts. Famous singers and bands from domestic to international often perform here. So whether you like traditional or contemporary music, there’s something for everyone.
