雅思口语机经案例训练

2026/4/7 雅思 雅思 口语

此篇为雅思口语的机经案例训练

Part 1:

必问题：work or study
  • What subjects are you studying?
    • I’m studying Big Data at college.
    • It’s really practical because we learn things like data analysis that keep me up with tech trends.
    • Plus, having these skills makes me more competitive when I look for jobs later.
  • Do you like your subject?
    • Yeah, I really love my Big Data major.
    • It’s super engaging because we get to work on real-world projects, like analyzing social media trends.
    • I also think it’s really practical, since data skills are in such high demand right now. This makes me feel more confident about finding a good job after graduation.
  • Why did you choose to study that subject?
    • I chose Big Data mainly for career reasons.
    • When I was picking my major, I noticed almost every industry from tech to traditional was hiring data experts.
    • The high demand and good salary prospects made it a practical choice.
    • Also, I’ve always liked working with numbers, so it felt like a good fit for my interests too.
  • Do you think that your subject is popular in your country？
    • Absolutely, Big Data is super popular in my country.
    • A lot of universities have been increasing their enrollment for Big Data programs over the past few years.
    • I also see more and more of my peers choosing this major because of the growing job opportunities.
    • Everywhere from tech firms to traditional companies are hiring data specialists,
    • and there’s constant coverage in the news about how data skills are essential now.
  • Do you have any plans for your studies in the next five years？
    • Over the next five years, I have a clear study plan.
    • First, I’ll focus on maintaining strong academic performance in my remaining college years and earn industry certifications like Google Data Analytics.
    • After graduating, I plan to pursue a master’s degree in Data Science abroad to deepen my expertise.
    • Once I finish my postgraduate studies, I’ll return to my country to work in a Big Data role, aiming for career growth in fields like data consulting or AI research.
  • What are the benefits of being your age?
    • Being in my 20s helps me have more freedom to make my own decisions when comparing to the younger generation, like picking my major or studying abroad.
    • Also, compared to elder adults, I’ve got more energy to learn new skills without feeling drained.
    • And since I don’t have as many responsibilities yet, I can take risks, like switching career path that older people with families might not be able to.
  • Do you want to change your major？
    • Not really. I’m majoring in Big Data, and it’s super practical.
    • It keeps up with the latest tech trends, which helps me stay competitive in the job market.
    • I think it has a really bright future and I also genuinely like it. So I’ve never thought about switching majors.
  • Do you prefer to study in the mornings or in the afternoons？
    • I definitely prefer studying in the afternoons.
    • I usually stay up late, like until 2 or 3 AM, so I wake up late and have zero energy in the mornings.
    • But after lunch, I fully recharge and get really energetic. That’s when I head to the library to focus on my studies.
  • How much time do you spend on your studies each week？
    • I haven’t tracked it exactly,
    • but I go to the library every day to study. On average, I spend about 5 to 6 hours there each afternoon and evening, when I’m free from classes.
    • But if I have a really busy day with lots of intense courses, I study less—maybe only a couple of hours that day.
  • Are you looking forward to working？
    • Absolutely! I can’t wait to start my career in Big Data.
    • Learning through real practical projects is way more effective than just studying in class.
    • Also, I’m excited to earn my own salary instead of relying on my parents for study fees.
    • Plus, working will give me more freedom to balance my energy between learning new things and my free time.
  • What technology do you use when you study?
    • As I major in big data, coding languages are essential for me like python and C# to hep me meet the assignment requirements, especially for data processing tasks.
    • I also relying on visualization tools such as excel and power bi to present my analysis results clearly.
    • When I get stuck on my coding problems, I also use AI tools to get guidance and improve my code to fit my teachers’ requirement.
  • What changes would you like to see in your school?
    • For my Big Data major, I really hope my school can upgrade the tech facilities.
    • Right now, the data analysis software is a bit outdated and the computers are slow, which makes my projects take longer than they should.
    • I also wish we had more partnerships and cooperation with big data companies. Because getting to learn real-world skills from industry experts would be a more effective way than just reading textbooks.
  • What do you think is the most important at the moment?
    • Right now, maintaining strong academic performance is my top priority because good grades are essential for my future plans.
    • Because I’m preparing to apply for a postgraduate degree in Big Data, as I want to specialize deeper in this field.
    • I’m also working hard to earn industry certifications in data analysis, like Tableau or Python, which will make me more competitive.
  • Who helps you the most? And how?
    • My computer science university teacher helps me the most.
    • He doesn’t just teach the basic tech for the course, also gives me one-on-one guidance to dive deeper into specific areas I’m interested in.
    • On top of that, he helps me plan my future career and guides me to get useful industry certifications, which will boost my job prospects later.
大概率问题：城市

  • What city do you live in?

    • I live in Shanghai, a famous city in eastern China right next to the sea. It’s a major business center where lots of people come for work and career opportunities. It also has countless famous tourist spots and rich cultural heritage. You can explore ancient Chinese art at the Shanghai Museum, walk along the Bund to see colonial European buildings alongside modern skyscrapers. For food, Nanjing Road has everything from local snacks to nice restaurants. So that 's the reasons why millions of visitors from home and abroad come to Shanghai every years.

  • Do you like this city? Why?（背一背，新颖）

    • Yes, absolutely. I’ve lived in Shanghai my whole life, and it holds precious memories as it’s my hometown. What I love most, though, is that even after all these years, there’s always something new to discover, especially for art lovers like me. The city hosts regular exhibitions featuring artists from all over the world, with personal shows ranging from traditional Chinese painting to cutting-edge digital art. These events perfectly match my passion for art and keep me excited to explore more of what my city has to offer.

  • Would you recommend your city to others?

    • Absolutely, I’d really recommend Shanghai. It’s one of China’s most famous cities. It’s not only a top business center but also has a lot of culture. You can visit great museums like the Shanghai Museum, or walk along the Bund to see old European buildings next to modern skyscrapers. For food, Nanjing Road is a must - try. There you can find popular food from local snacks to some decent restaurants. For many foreigners, it’s the first city they visit in China because it mixes old and new so well. Whether you’re traveling for fun, work, or to learn about culture, there’s something for everyone here.

  • What’s the weather like where you live?

    • Shanghai has pretty good weather most of the year. Since it’s near the sea, the temperature is usually mild. But we have two extreme seasons: January and February are quite cold, and July to August get really hot. Most days are sunny or cloudy, and we don’t have too much rain except during the rainy season in June. That time is tough because it’s hot and super humid, with non - stop light rain lasting weeks to months. But generally speaking, the weather is relvatively pleasant and I think that’s the reason why so many people choose to live here.

  • Are there people of different ages living in this city?

    • Yes, Shanghai has a mix of all age groups. Adults make up the biggest part because so many people move here for work—they’re the city’s main workforce. There are also more and more elderly people now. Many choose to retire here because of the good healthcare, nice weather, and government support for seniors. As for kids and teenagers, their numbers have gone down in recent years. Fewer couples are having children these days, so there are not as many young people as before. Overall, though, you can find people of every age living in Shanghai.

  • Are the people friendly in the city?

    • Honestly, I don’t think people in Shanghai are as friendly as they used to be. These days, few people talk to strangers on the street. When you asked for directions, today many people just walk past without even looking up. I think the main reason is the high pressure of city life. Everyone is busy with their own work, so they don’t have energy to help others. People focus more on themselves now, which makes the city feel a little bit less warm.

  • Is the city friendly to children and old people?

    • Not really. Like I said before, people here are too busy with their own lives to pay much attention to others. For the elderly, there are more and more news stories about seniors faking falls to trick people into giving them money. This makes young people scared to help old folks in need. For children, most people think taking care of kids is only the parents’ job. So they won’t step in to help or even smile at other people’s children. It’s a shame because both groups really need more kindness and everyone will get old in the future.

  • Do you often see your neighbors?

    • Oh yeah, I see my neighbor every weekend without fail. She’s an elderly lady who works as a volunteer for our neiborhood committee. Her job involves helping neighbors get along better and delivering daily supplies to older people who can’t take care of themselves. Since I’m busy with my study and have to stay on campus during weekdays, I only come home on weekends. That’s when I always meet her—she’s usually busy with her community work.
话题题：
开头：
  • 事件类：Once / Last year / When I was a child + 直接跟故事
  • 人物类：I’d like to talk about 名字，who
  • 将来：If possible / If I got a chance
1、Views
  • Do you like taking pictures of different views?
    • Absolutely, I’m really into taking photos of natural scenery, especially when I’m traveling to other countries. These photos help me to record some meaningful moments. Whenever I look back at them in the future, I can recall the feelings and experiences from that trip.
  • Do you prefer views in urban areas or rural areas?
    • I definitely prefer rural areas. Since I live in a city, I’m totally fed up with the endless tall buildings and heavy traffic. While rural places have a more peaceful, natural atmosphere—with green grass, various plants, and even some small animals around. And it can also help me to escape from the stressful, fast - paced city life I usually lead.
  • Do you prefer views in your own country or in other countries?
    • I actually prefer views in other countries. When I travel abroad with friends, we get to experience totally different cultures and landscapes, like the cherry blossom season in Japan I have just been to. These unique experiences feel more memorable than the views back home, which I’ve grown a bit used to over time.
  • Have you seen an unforgettable and beautiful view or scenery?
    • I will just talk about the cherry blossom view in Kyoto, Japan is unforgettable. During my last vacation, I went to Japan with friends. There was very dense cherry trees in that tourism spot, and the pink cherry blossoms fell like snow, covering the stone path and the river. We sat on a bench there, drinking teas and watching people wearing traditional costumes taking photos. It’s a memoriable experience.
2、childhood activities

  • What are your favourite activities?

    • My favourite activity is definitely playing basketball. I usually invite my friends to play together in my spare time. I really enjoy the competitive atmosphere of the game, and it’s also an excellent way for me to unwind and relieve pressure after a long day.

  • What were your favourite activities when you were a child?

    • I was a huge fan of basketball at a young age. My mum even signed me up for a basketball training class.And I also remember once represented my class in the basketball matches at school.

  • Did you prefer to do activities alone or with a group of people when you were a child?

    • I definitely preferred doing activities with a group when I was a kid. It felt much more fun and lively when I was with other people. I also really enjoyed the sense of both cooperation and competition.

  • Are there any differences between the activities you liked when you were a child and those you like now?

    • Not really, there aren’t many big differences. I’ve always loved playing basketball.

      But as I’ve grown older, I have much less time to play with my friends. On one hand, I often lack energy, and on the other hand, everyone is busy with their own lives so we can hardly get together. Instead, we usually watch basketball matches online together now.

3、buildings
  • Do you take photos of buildings?
    • Definitely, I think every building has its own unique style and design, so I really enjoy taking photos of their special shapes and structures.
  • Is there a building that you would like to visit?
    • Yes, I really want to visit the Forbidden City in Beijing. I’m deeply attracted by its rich culture heritage and the special traditional architectural style. During my last visit to Beijin, I’ve forgot to make a reservation in advance, and the tickets were sold out at scene, so I definitely will pay another visit to it one day.
  • Do you want to live in a tall building?
    • Yes, I do. I think living in a tall building is great because the view from up high is really good. I can overlook the whole city, and it’s also very enjoyable to feel the wind on a high floor as a way to relax myself.
  • Are there tall buildings near your home?
    • Yes, there are quite a lot of tall buildings near my home. I live in the city centre, so high-rise buildings are everywhere around my neighbourhood.
4、scenery
  • Do you prefer the mountains or the sea?
    • I definitely prefer the sea. To be honest, mountain climbing is quite tiring, and you have to reach the very top to enjoy the nice views. By contrast, the view along the shore is already very stunning. I can just lie on the beach, enjoy the sunshine, or watch the sunset over the sea.
  • Do you like to take scenery pictures?
    • Yes, I really enjoy taking photos of beautiful scenery, especially when I travel abroad to other countries. It’s a great way to record amazing views and keep wonderful memories from my trips.
  • Do you look out the window at the scenery when travelling by bus or car?
    • Yes, I do. I sometimes get carsickness, so I have to open the window and look at the scenery outside to stop myself from feeling sick.（晕车）
  • What are the most beautiful sights you have seen while travelling?
    • The most beautiful scenery I’ve ever seen was cherry blossoms in Japan. During last spring break, I visited Japan and saw cherry blossoms falling like snow onto the stone paths. It was such an unforgettable scene, and I still remember it clearly to this day.

Part 2：

话题一：自学 &
题目：

Describe one of your friends who learned something without a teacher

参考点：
  • Who he/she is
  • What he/she learned
  • Why he/she learned this
  • And explain whether it would be easier to learn from a teacher
观点：

I’d like to talk about my friend Jenny Chen, who was my deskmate in high school.

Three years ago, she was getting ready to pursue her undergraduate degree in the UK, and that was also when she taught herself how to cook completely on her own, without any teacher.

The skill she learned was cooking, including making daily meals and baking. The reason she decided to do this was very practical. We all knew that eating out in the UK was quite expensive, and decent restaurants usually cost a lot. Compared to that, buying fresh ingredients and cooking for herself was far more economical. So right after she finished the college entrance exam three years ago, she started learning to cook by herself.

I still remember the first time I visited her home after the exam. She offered me some cookies she had baked on her own. To be honest, at that time, I didn’t think she have any talent in cooking. The cookies were dry and overly sweet, and they tasted totally terrible.

However, after just one summer holiday, she invited me over for dinner again. Her cooking skills had improved dramatically. She was really good at stir-frying dishes, controlling the heat, and seasoning food just right. The meal she prepared was actually delicious.

She later told me that she had learned recipes and useful cooking tips from online videos and food websites, and practised constantly. I really admired her for mastering such a useful skill in such a short time all by herself.

As for whether it would have been easier to learn from a teacher, I personally believed that self-learning was more efficient. Since we can could make suitable plans based on our own level, and at own pace, and there was no pressure from a teacher. That made the whole learning process more relaxing and enjoyable.

话题二：乐于助人
题目：Describe a person who often helps others
参考点：
  • Who this person is
  • How often he/she helps others
  • How/why he/she helps others
  • And how you feel about this person
观点：

I’d like to talk about my neighbour, Mrs Lin. She’s a retired elderly lady who lives right next door to my family, and we’ve been neighbours ever since I was born.

After retirement, she works as a community volunteer. Her jobs mainly include organise regular community events and tries to build a friendlier, more connected environment among all the residents.

I still remember a period of time when an elderly couple living downstairs needed help. Their children had to go away on business trips, and the old couple found it difficult to go out and shop for daily things. So they asked Mrs Lin if she could help buy vegetables and groceries for them. She agreed without hesitation. Every day when we walked upstairs, we could see fresh vegetables hanging on their doorknob, brought over by Mrs Lin. She was really warm-hearted and responsible.

Apart from that, in her daily life, she also makes an effort to look after elderly people who live alone in our neighbourhood. She brings them daily newspapers, sits and chats with them, and keeps them company so they don’t feel lonely. She does all this out of kindness, not because she has to.

I truly admire and respect Mrs Lin from the bottom of my heart. She sets a really good example for everyone around her, and I think it’s wonderful to have such a kind and helpful person living nearby.

话题三：不享受的音乐活动
题目：

Describe an event you attended in which you didn’t enjoy the music played

参考点：
  • What it was
  • Who you went with
  • Why you decided to go there
  • And explain why you didn’t enjoy it
观点：

I want to talk about a university freshman orientation event where I think the music didn’t fit the atmosphere that it should be. It was my first year of college, and the school organized this event aiming to help new students meet and make friends. I went with two of my roommates. Initially, I thought it would be a calm, relaxed place where we could chat easily and learn about each other’s hobbies and that’s the reason why I chose to go. But when we arrived, the background music was so loud and annoying. They were playing heavy rock and upbeat pop songs, which totally contradicted with the need for quiet conversation. Everyone had to shout just to be heard, and after 10 minutes, my friends and I were all exhausted from this kind of communication style. We ended up leaving early, grabbing some other students we’d just met, and going to a quiet coffee shop nearby to actually talk and make friends properly. Looking back, I still can’t believe how bad the music choice was for that kind of event.

话题四：发挥想象力 & in a smart way（没蜡烛了）
题目：

Describe a time you needed to use your imagination

&

参考点：
  • When it was
  • Why you needed to use imagination
  • How difficult or easy it was
  • And explain how you felt about it
观点：

I wanna talk about the birthday cake I made for my mom last year. I used my imagination during the process and the decoration.

I didn’t want to buy a plain cake from a shop. So I spent the whole afternoon making one myself.

I followed a tutorial online step by step. It was way harder than it looked! The cream was really tricky to handle—it kept melting. And I had to watch the oven very carefully because the baking time was super strict. If it was even a little off, the cake would get burnt. But I finally made it work.

Then I added my own ideas. I used my favorite colors and drew little hearts and stars on it. I also put on some birthday candles and a small photo of us from when I was little.

When she saw it, she was so happy and surprised. She said my imagination turned a simple cake into the most meaningful gift she’d ever had.

This made me see: imagination can turn small things into something really special and full of love.

话题五：电子产品
题目：

Describe a piece of technology (not a phone) that you would like to own

参考点：
  • What it is
  • How much it costs
  • How you knew it
  • And explain why you would like to own it
观点：

I’d like to talk about a pair of Bose over-ear headphones that I really hope to own in the near future.

The main reason I want a new pair is that my current headphones are getting really old and worn out. They charge really slowly these days, and the battery life has dropped significantly. I often study in the school library for hours on end, and I rely on headphones to block out noise and stay focused while listening to music. So a reliable battery life is really important for me.

I’ve known this brand really well for a long time, because my current headphones are also made by Bose. In fact, they were a birthday present from my dad a few years ago, and that’s why I trust this brand.

The price is around 1,000 yuan, which is roughly 140 US dollars. To be honest, it’s not cheap for me—it’s almost one-third of my monthly living expenses. But I’m willing to save up for it because I trust this brand a lot.

What’s more, my old Bose headphones have served me really well for years. That’s why I’m looking forward to the new model.

All in all, this piece of technology would make my study experience much more comfortable and efficient, and that’s why I really want to own it.

话题六：爱护自然
题目：

Describe a person who likes to look after the natural world

参考点：
  • Who this person is
  • What he or she does
  • How he or she does it
  • How often he or she does it
  • And explain how you feel about this person
观点：

I’d like to talk about my mom, who is the person I know that loves taking care of the natural world the most.

First, she really likes growing plants. She has many flowers and green plants on our balcony, like roses. Different seasons have different flowers, so she often takes me to the flower market near our home to pick up new plants. It’s a great opportunity for me to see a large range of rare breeds, which is really fun.

Besides, she’s also a huge fan of going to the park. Every weekend, she meets her friends at the park close to our house. They sometimes take a walk around the lake, chat about their daily lives, or just sit there and enjoy a cup of coffee together, surrounded by trees and fresh air.

Recently, she’s picked up a new hobby: keeping fish. Last week, she bought some small tropical fish and spent almost a whole afternoon making a nice home for them, including cleaning the tank doing some decorations and adjusting the water temperature. Now she does this kind of things almost everyday.

I really admire my mom. She shows me how to love and take care of nature in simple, daily ways. She makes our home full of life with her plants and fish, and she also teaches me to slow down and enjoy the beauty of the natural world around us.

话题七：城市 & 旅游
题目：

Describe a city you enjoyed visiting

&

Describe a city that you think is very interesting/famous

参考点：
  • Where it is
  • When you visited it
  • How long you stayed there
  • What you did there
  • And explain why you enjoyed visiting it

&

  • Where it is
  • What it is famous for
  • How you knew this city
  • And explain why you think it is very interesting/famous
观点：

I want to talk about Xi’an, a city I really enjoyed visiting.

First, Xi’an is located in central China, and it’s one of the most historic cities in the country. It was the capital of many ancient Chinese dynasties, which is why it’s packed with incredible historical sites.

I visited it just a few months ago, during my winter holiday, together with my college roommates. We stayed there for 9 full days, which gave us plenty of time to explore the city properly.

During our stay, we did a lot of fun things.

First, we visited the Terracotta Army. It’s one of the Eight Wonders of the world, and also the tomb of Qin Shi Huang, the first emperor of China. It was so impressive to see all the clay soldiers stand up close.

Besides, we tried lots of local food. My favorite was cold noodles, called Liangpi. It’s totally different from the noodles in other parts of China, and it was super delicious.

We also walked around the city and saw many people wearing traditional Tang Dynasty costumes taking photos on the street. And it was a really nice view.

I loved this city so much for many reasons.

First, I learned a lot about ancient China in different museums.

Second, the local food is fantastic, and the people in Xi’an are very friendly and warm. They always helped us when we asked for directions and recommendations, which made us feel at home.

It was a perfect trip, and I can’t wait to pay a visit again.

话题九：想要的工作
题目：

Describe a perfect job you would like to have in the future

参考点：
  • What it is
  • How you knew it
  • What you need to learn to get this job
  • And explain why you think it is a perfect job for you
观点：

I’d like to talk about the perfect job I want to have in the future—it’s a data analyst position at Microsoft. It has been my dream job for a long time, and it’s the main goal that drives me to study hard every day.

The main reasons I’m so interested in this job are quite clear. First, it is a perfect match for my major, and second, it offers a highly competitive salary and excellent future career development. These two factors alone make it really stand out to me.

I first learned about this job when I was making my career plan right after I enrolled in university. I’m majoring in data science, so I researched many well-known companies in this field, and Microsoft immediately caught my attention. As a world-famous tech giant, it has an excellent reputation, and I use its products almost every day—Power BI and Excel are essential tools for data analysts, and Power Automate helps with simple task automation. Using these practical tools made me more eager to work for the company that created them, and I hope to be part of the team that uses data to solve real-world problems.

To get this job, I know I need to keep improving my skills. Currently, I’m focusing on mastering the core courses of my major, such as statistics, data visualization, and programming languages like Python and SQL—these are the basics for a qualified data analyst. Besides, I’ve actively obtained relevant certificates for Power BI and Excel to deepen my understanding of Microsoft’s tools and prove my practical abilities. I also plan to do an internship in a data-related role later, to gain real work experience and get familiar with the daily work of a data analyst.

Beyond the salary and major match, I also think this job is incredibly meaningful and motivating. Working in such an innovative company will allow me to learn from top professionals in the industry, which will help me become more excellent in my field.

This job combines my major, interests and future development needs, so I believe it’s the perfect job for me. I will keep working hard to improve myself and make this dream come true.

话题十：喜欢画画的孩子
题目：

Describe a child who loves drawing/painting

参考点：
  • Who he/she is
  • How/when you knew him/her
  • How often he/she draws/paints
  • And explain why you think he/she loves drawing/painting
观点：

I’d like to talk about someone I used to know who really loves painting. She’s my little cousin, and she’s extremely talented at drawing, especially traditional Chinese painting, including ink wash painting and classical Chinese art.

Even when she was little, she’ve already showed a strong interest in calligraphy with a writing brush. That really helped her build a solid foundation for traditional Chinese painting from an early age, like how to control ink and use different brush techniques. I remember she even won several prizes in painting and calligraphy competitions.

We didn’t keep in touch much during those years when she went to middle schools and high schools, as she was busy at her study. But we met again in last summer vacation after she just passed the college entrance exam. She got into an art university, majoring in cultural relics restoration, which includes restoring ancient paintings, calligraphy works and ceramics.

I still remenber the time I took her to the museums and art galleries. She could spend hours looking at every single exhibit, trying to understand the styles and the stories behind them. To be honest, it felt quite boring for me at that time, since I didn’t have the same interest in art as she did. But I could clearly see how absorbed she was in paintings.

As I’ve heard recently, she’s now interning at an auction company, which still connects closely to art and paintings.

I really admire her for sticking with this interest for so many years. What’s more, she has turned her passion into something meaningful and is now contributing to the protection and preservation of traditional culture.

话题十：app
题目：

Describe a program or app on your computer or phone

参考点：
  • How often you use it
  • When/how you use it
  • When/how you found it

话题一：乐于助人
1、Do you think schools should teach children to do household chores?（做家务）

Yes, I definitely think schools should teach children to do household chores.

Doing physical chores regularly can keep students moving and help them stay fit, instead of sitting at desks all day.

When children know how to help with housework, they can share the burden with their parents and create a more harmonious family atmosphere.

Learning basic life skills at a young age also helps them become more responsible and organised people.

These practical abilities will prepare them better for living alone when they grow up and leave home.

2、Why are employees reluctant to ask their managers for help?

They may fear that asking questions will make them look unprofessional or inexperienced in front of their leaders.

Some people also worry that their managers will think they are not capable enough for their job.

Also, many employees don’t want to disturb their busy bosses with small problems.

3、What can children do to help their parents?

Doing well at school and keeping their grades up is probably the biggest help, because it makes parents feel proud and reassured that their efforts aren’t wasted.

When kids focus on their studies, parents don’t have to spend extra energy worrying about their future.

On top of that, helping with daily chores around the house can really reduce the family’s workload.

Simple tasks like tidying rooms can save parents a lot of stress after a long day.

4、Should children help their parents with household chores?

Sharing family responsibilities is something every kid should learn to do from an early age.

By helping out with daily chores, they can make their parents feel less tired and more relaxed after work.

At the same time, doing housework gives children a good chance to develop practical life skills.

It also helps them become more independent and understand how much effort their parents put in.

5、What kind of help do people need when looking for a new job?

First, many people need practical advice on writing a good resume or preparing for job interviews.（简历）

Some may also want information about suitable job openings or which industries are hiring more people.（招聘信息）

It’s also helpful to get emotional support from friends or family, especially when they feel stressed or discouraged.（情感支持，鼓励）

Besides, professional guidance on career choices can help them make better decisions for their future.（职业发展规划课程）

6、Who should people ask for help, colleagues or family members?

I think people should ask for help from both colleagues and family members.

Colleagues usually have professional experience, so they can give useful advice about the job market and interviews.

Family members, on the other hand, can offer strong emotional support when we feel stressed or unsure.

话题二：自学
1、Is it necessary to keep learning after graduating from school?

What we learn at school only makes up a small part of our lifelong knowledge, so we can’t rely on it for the rest of our lives.

The job market and technology are always changing, and continuous learning helps us stay competitive at work.

It also allows us to develop new skills and explore different interests, making our lives more fulfilling.（生活更加colorful）

2、Should teachers make learning in their classes fun?

When classes are enjoyable, students stay more focused and interested instead of feeling bored or sleepy.

This positive attitude not only improves their grades but also helps them build a lifelong love for learning.

Besides, fun and relaxed classes can reduce stress and make students more confident in expressing their ideas.（减少压力）

3、Do you think there are too many subjects for students to learn?

The modern workplace tends to value specialists who really master one skill, rather than generalists with only basic knowledge.

Having to study so many different subjects means students can’t focus deeply on the fields they actually enjoy or are good at.

This kind of broad learning doesn’t really match what the job market and society actually need now.

4、Is it better to focus on a few subjects or to learn many subjects?

As I mentioned earlier, people who specialise in one field are more likely to achieve better career development nowadays.

Focusing deeply allows us to put more time and energy into understanding a subject really well.

This kind of concentrated learning can inspire creativity and help people reach higher achievements.

In contrast, learning too many topics only leads to shallow knowledge and less real progress.

5、Do you think enterprises should provide training for their employees?

Professional training helps workers learn practical skills in a structured way, which greatly improves their work efficiency and performance.

It also gives employees a break from daily routines and acts as a form of refreshment at work.

Besides, training makes staff feel valued by the company, which can increase their motivation and loyalty.（新颖点）

Moreover, well-trained employees help the company stay competitive and adapt quickly to industry changes.（当下技术变化的快，使员工跟上科技发展，使公司维持竞争力）

6、Do you think it is good for older adults to continue learning?

By learning modern technologies such as smartphones and digital services, they can fully enjoy the convenience brought by the digital age.

Furthermore, it helps them maintain closer connections with younger generations and prevents them from feeling isolated.（跟年轻人关系更紧密，避免孤单）

Taking up new hobbies like gardening or photography also enables them to enrich their retirement life and make it more fulfilling.

Continuous learning keeps their brains active and sharp, which is beneficial to their mental health.（动脑子健康）
