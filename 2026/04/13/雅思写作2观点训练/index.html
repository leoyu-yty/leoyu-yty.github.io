二、观点积累

1）溺爱孩子

题目：

Some parents give their children everything that their children ask for or allow them to do whatever they want to do. Is this good for children? What could be consequences for these children when they grow up?

观点：
  • In my opinion, this way of raising children is bad for their growth and will cause negative results when they become adults.
问题一：Is this good for children?
  • children who are too spoiled will lose the ability to take care of themselves and become too dependent on others.
  • Besides, spoiling children too much reduces their chances to learn practical skills and a sense of responsibility.
问题二：What could be consequences for these children when they grow up?
  • spoiled children are not competitive at work.
    • not used to difficulties or failures
    • as they are used to getting what they want and don’t know how to cooperate
  • they may have mental problems like anxiety and low self-esteem

2）科技对老年人的帮助

题目：

Mobile phones and the internet bring a lot of benefits to people nowadays. However, older people use them the least. In what aspects do mobile phones and the internet benefit older people? How can we encourage them to use these technologies?

观点：
  • These digital tools substantially benefit seniors, and effective methods can be taken to encourage their adoption.
问题一：In what aspects do mobile phones and the internet benefit older people?
  • 总起句：Digital technology enhances elderly people’s well-being in three key ways.

  • it improves personal safety

    • monitor their elders’ position, reducing the risk of getting lost

  • it helps strengthen family bonds

    • Video calls and instant messages
    • alleviating loneliness and boosting mental health

  • enriches retirement life by providing entertainment

问题二：How can we encourage them to use these technologies?
  • 总起句：To promote digital technology use among the elderly, practical solutions exist.

  • enroll their parents in elderly universities

  • communities can arrange one-on-one assistance by recruiting volunteers to solve real-life problems for the elderly

  • For technology companies, they can make innovations and design a more simple and user-friendly product

3）Who Should Be Responsible for Transporting Children to School?（政府还是家长）

题目：Some people say it is government’s responsibility to transport children to school, while others believe parents should transport their children to school. Discuss both views and give your opinion.
观点:
  • I strongly believe that parents should take the main duty in sending and picking up their kids
观点一：政府应该负责接送孩子
  • 总起句：Admittedly, the government should bear some responsibility for ensuring children’s safety and convenience on their way to and from school.

  • the government should provide alternative transportation options for parents

  • some families cannot afford private transportation

  • for some students who live far away from school, the government-provided accommodation near schools can be a suitable option

观点二：家长应该负责接送孩子
  • 总起句：On the other hand, parents should take the main responsibility for transporting their children to and from school.

  • it is much safer for parents to complete this task themselves

  • parents have a legal obligation to take care of their children

  • prevent their children from coming into contact with strangers and potential risks

  • family bonds can be strengthened through daily transportation

    • less likely to suffer from hidden mental illnesses

4）保护环境（原因+解决方案）

题目：There are many environmental problems, such as the greenhouse effect and climate change, and people have hardly found any solutions. Why is this the case? And what measures can be taken?
观点：
  • This phenomenon stems from several interrelated factors, and there are practical strategies that individuals and governments can adopt to make tangible progress in addressing these issues.
问题一：Why is this the case?
  • 总起句：The difficulty in solving environmental problems can be mainly attributed to two key factors.

  • countries around the world have failed to make joint efforts to tackle such a challenge.（国际合作）

  • the effects of environmental protection measures are usually not immediately noticeable in the short term（短期效果不明显）

问题二：And what measures can be taken?
  • 总起句：However, there are several practical methods for both individuals and governments to address these pressing problems.

  • environmental publicity and education should be enhanced to raise public awareness（宣传+教育）

    • be more willing to cooperate with relevant policies
    • making it easier to implement further environmental protection measures such as waste classification

  • more funds and support should be invested in environmentally-related programs（投入资金）

    • allocate more financial resources to cultivate professionals
    • international cooperation should be strengthened
      • developed countries providing financial and technical assistance to developing nations
备注：
  • environmental problems=environmental issues
  • have been solved = have been put forward
  • was driven by=stem from
  • substantial大量的
  • 中途：midway
  • 对抗：combating environmental problems=tackle with

  • conduct research
  • 宣传：publicity / awareness
  • 强调：be enhanced to raise public awareness
  • nature做自然意思时前面不加the

5）体育赛事的性别差异（原因+原因）

题目：
Today, TV channels provide men’s sports shows more than women’s sports shows. Why? Should TV channels give equal time for women’s sports and men’s sports?
观点：
  • This phenomenon is driven by several key factors, and I firmly believe TV channels should provide equal airtime for both men’s and women’s sports.
问题一：Why?
  • 总起句：The unbalanced airtime distribution between men’s and women’s sports shows can be mainly attributed to three key reasons.
  • men’s sports are more appealing and boast a large global audience（观众多）
    • to cater to the massive audience demand
  • men’s sports have more frequent match schedules（比赛更多）
    • while women’s leagues are relatively fewer and have less frequent games, making it harder for TV channels to arrange regular programs for them
  • from a business perspective, broadcasting men’s sports is more profitable（值）
    • attract more sponsors and well-known athletes, who have strong appeal to advertisers
问题二：Should TV channels give equal time for women’s sports and men’s sports?
  • 总起句：Despite the reasons mentioned above, I firmly believe that TV channels should give equal time to women’s and men’s sports, for two compelling reasons.
  • recent years have witnessed a significant growth in the number of outstanding female athletes and the popularity of women’s sports（越来越受欢迎）
  • women’s sports have enormous commercial potential（商业价值）
    • Female athletes have unique advantages in promoting products related to clothing, cosmetics and fitness,

6）儿童犯错的自由与管控

题目：
Some people think children have the freedom to make mistakes, while other people believe that adults should prevent children from making mistakes. Discuss both sides and give your opinion.
观点：
  • From my perspective, letting children make errors in their life is much more beneficial for their long-term development.
问题一：other people believe that adults should prevent children from making mistakes
  • 总起句：Admittedly, it is reasonable for adults to prevent children from making mistakes and guide them to find proper answers.
  • it is a much more efficient way to let children know the correct answer
    • saves valuable time for learning other things
  • preventing children from making mistakes can protect them from physical or psychological harm（新颖点-安全）
    • Adults’ intervention can avoid such adverse consequences and help children grow up in a safer and more stable environment
  • guiding children to avoid mistakes can help them form good habits from an early age（新颖点-habits）
    • establish correct values and norms
问题二：Some people think children have the freedom to make mistakes
  • 总起句：While the benefits of letting children learn from mistakes by themselves are far more significant.
  • granting children the freedom to make mistakes can boost their creativity and innovation
    • be more willing to try new things, instead of just following adults’ instructions blindly
    • which is crucial for their future study and career development.
  • through learning from mistakes by themselves, children can have a deeper impression of the correct methods and avoid repeating the same mistakes.
    • are more memorable and can help children form their own problem-solving abilities
  • making mistakes and correcting them can cultivate children’s resilience and sense of responsibility（新颖点-性格）

7）成人扫盲教育的重要性

题目：
Education of young people is highly prioritized in many countries. However, educating adults who cannot write or read is even more important and governments should spend more money on this. To what extent do you agree or disagree?
观点：
  • From my perspective, although this special group of adults deserves attention and support, it is not advisable to prioritize their education over that of young people or spend excessive money on it.
问题一：educating adults who cannot write or read is even more important and governments should spend more money on this
  • 总起句：Admittedly, strengthening the education of illiterate adults is necessary and brings certain social and personal benefits.
  • helping more illiterate adults improve their literacy skills can expand the quality of the labor force（劳动力质量的提升）
    • Illiterate adults often face limited employment opportunities and can only engage in low-paying, labor-intensive work
    • By acquiring basic literacy, they can access better jobs, increase their income, and thus improve their quality of life.
  • improving adults’ literacy can reduce social inequality（避免社会不公平）
    • Illiterate adults cannot read newspapers, use digital devices, or understand public notices, which prevents them from fully engaging in social activities
  • educating illiterate adults can promote social stability（提高社会稳定性）
问题二：Education of young people is highly prioritized
  • 总起句：investing more resources in younger generations is far more beneficial to long-term economic growth and social development
  • young people are the future driving force of a country’s development（年轻人是未来社会驱动力）
    • Unlike illiterate adults, who have limited room for skill improvement and a shorter working cycle, young people have a longer learning period and stronger learning ability
  • prioritizing young people’s education is a more cost-effective investment for governments（值）
    • investing in young people’s education can generate long-term returns
    • young people can pass on their knowledge and skills to future generations, forming a virtuous cycle of education and development.

8）家庭教育替代学校教育

题目：

In some countries, there has been an increase in the number of parents who educate their children themselves at home instead of sending them to school. Do you think the advantages of this outweigh the disadvantages?

观点：
  • While this approach offers certain benefits in specific scenarios, I firmly believe that its drawbacks far outweigh its advantages.
问题一：
  • 总起句：Admittedly, educating children at home has several merits that contribute to children’s short-term growth and family convenience.
  • the one-on-one teaching mode enables parents to tailor the learning content and pace to their children’s individual needs
  • home education can reduce both time and financial expenditures for families
  • home education can provide a safer and more comfortable learning environment
问题二：
  • 总起句：Despite the above benefits, home education exerts more negative impacts on children’s long-term development, especially in terms of their personality cultivation, social skills, and academic comprehensiveness.
  • children educated at home are likely to become introverted and even suffer from mental health issues
  • keeping children away from group learning environments deprives them of the chance to develop essential social skills and a sense of responsibility
  • most parents lack professional teaching qualifications and resources, which may lead to incomplete or unbalanced academic education

9）机器人

题目：

Today, there are many tasks at home or work that are done by robots. Is this a positive or negative development?

观点：

Although some people claim that this trend may bring negative effects to human life, I firmly believe that the benefits it offers far outweigh the drawbacks.

问题一：

  • 总起句：Admittedly, the widespread use of robots involves certain potential risks.

  • On one hand, the unemployment rate may continue to rise, leaving many workers unable to support themselves financially.

    As machines replace human labor, public anxiety may grow over whether robots will eventually take over all jobs and even dominate human society.

  • On the other hand, over-reliance on robots can negatively affect people’s physical health and creative abilities.

    Since many manual tasks are performed by machines, people may lack physical exercise and become more likely to suffer from obesity. In addition, in technology companies, automated systems reduce the need for creative problem-solving, which may gradually weaken employees’ innovative thinking.

问题二：

  • 总起句：Despite these concerns, the application of robots provides substantial advantages to individuals and society as a whole.

  • For households, robots can undertake tedious and physically demanding housework, freeing people from repetitive chores and allowing them to save time and energy.

    With basic labor handled automatically, people can focus more on creative activities and personal development.

  • For industries, factories and high-tech enterprises can significantly improve work efficiency and productivity.

    Robots operate with greater accuracy and consistency than humans, enabling companies to increase output and generate higher profits. This efficiency also promotes economic growth and technological progress on a social level.

总结：
  • In conclusion, while the increasing use of robots may lead to job displacement and reduced physical activity, its positive impacts on efficiency, convenience and social development are far more significant. Therefore, I regard the rise of robots as a generally positive development.

10）广告

题目：

Today, the high sales of popular consumer goods reflect the power of advertising and not the real needs of the society in which they are sold. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

观点：
  • Nowadays, popular consumer goods often achieve high sales figures. Some people claim that this phenomenon mainly results from the influence of advertising rather than the actual needs of society.
  • I completely agree with this view to a large extent.
问题一：
  • 总起句：The high sales of popular consumer goods are primarily driven by the overwhelming power of advertising.
  • Firstly, advertising creates artificial demands that do not correspond to real social needs.
  • Secondly, repetitive advertising enhances brand exposure and strengthens consumers’ memory, making them more likely to purchase products unconsciously.
  • Thirdly, advertising exploits people’s psychological weaknesses, such as the desire to keep up with trends.
问题二：
  • 总起句：While advertising is the main driver, the actual needs of society do play a minor role in the high sales of some consumer goods.
  • To start with, basic daily necessities, such as food, clothing and household items, sell well because they meet people’s essential needs for survival and comfort.
  • Secondly, some innovative products that solve real-life problems gain high sales due to their practical value.
  • Thirdly, products that cater to specific groups’ needs, such as baby products for parents or fitness equipment for health-conscious people, achieve high sales because they meet targeted real demands

11）无人驾驶汽车的社会影响

题目：

It will be better to have wide use of driverless cars for individuals and society. To what extent do you agree or disagree with the statement?

观点：
  • Nowadays, many people argue that it is beneficial to promote the wide use of driverless cars to the public.
  • Although it has some certain drawbacks in several aspects, I firmly believe that its positive impacts far outweigh the negative ones.
问题一：

  • 总起句：Admittedly, the wide use of driverless cars can have some negative impacts.

  • firstly, it may have a negative impact on relevant laws and regulations. （法律）

    At present, most traffic laws are formulated based on human-driven vehicles, and the wide use of driverless cars will bring new legal issues—such as who should be responsible when an accident occurs (the car manufacturer, the AI developer or the car owner). It will take time and energy to revise the existing laws to adapt to this new form of transportation.

  • In addition, the popularization of driverless cars may cause unemployment in related industries. （失业）

  • A large number of people make a living by driving, such as taxi drivers, truck drivers and bus drivers. If driverless cars are widely used, these workers may lose their jobs, which will bring pressure to themselves and their families, and even affect social stability in a short time.

问题二：

  • 总起句：Despite the above disadvantages, the wide spread of driverless cars can bring significant benefits to both individuals and society.

  • For individuals, it can improve the efficiency of people’s life and work. （efficiency）

    Driverless cars can automatically plan the optimal route and avoid traffic jams, which saves people a lot of time spent on driving. For example, commuters can use the time during the journey to work, study or rest, instead of focusing on driving.

    In addition, it is more convenient for people who cannot drive, such as the elderly, the disabled and teenagers, to travel independently, which enhances their quality of life and sense of independence.

  • For society, the overall traffic accident rate can be greatly reduced. （安全）

    Unlike humans, AI drivers will not be affected by fatigue, distraction, drunk driving or emotional fluctuations, which are the main causes of most traffic accidents. With the wide use of driverless cars, the number of traffic accidents, injuries and deaths will decrease significantly, reducing social losses and the burden on medical and public security departments.

  • Moreover, driverless cars are more environmentally friendly and can help reduce environmental pollution. （环保）

    Most driverless cars are powered by clean energy (such as electricity), which produces no exhaust emissions. In addition, AI can control the speed and driving rhythm more reasonably, reducing fuel consumption and carbon emissions compared with human-driven cars. This is of great significance for coping with global climate change and building a low-carbon society.

总结：
  • In conclusion, although the wide use of driverless cars may bring some problems such as safety risks, legal challenges and temporary unemployment,
  • its benefits to individuals and society are more obvious—it improves people’s travel efficiency, reduces traffic accidents, protects the environment and optimizes urban resources.
  • Therefore, I fully agree that the wide use of driverless cars is better for both individuals and society.

12）国际合作

题目：

Some people think the main benefit of international cooperation is in the protection of the environment, while others think that the main benefits are in the world of business. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

观点：
  • Some people contend that the primary advantage of international collaboration lies in environmental preservation, while others argue that it is the business sector that reaps the most significant benefits.
  • Although global cooperation undoubtedly fosters economic growth and commercial development, I firmly believe that its contribution to environmental protection is far more crucial and far-reaching.
问题一：

  • 总起句：Admittedly, international cooperation serves as a powerful engine for global business prosperity.

  • Firstly, it facilitates the expansion of international trade, which in turn drives the evolution of transportation and logistics industries.

    By promoting cross-border import and export activities, local enterprises, particularly those in agriculture and manufacturing, can access broader markets, thereby generating substantial revenues and creating numerous job opportunities for the global workforce.

  • Furthermore, cooperation between major nations accelerates technological innovation and industrial advancement.

    Countries often join forces in high-tech domains such as aerospace and artificial intelligence. While some nations contribute research expertise, others provide essential facilities and financial funding, leading to breakthroughs that enhance overall industrial efficiency and competitiveness.

问题二：

  • 总起句：On the other hand, I firmly assert that international cooperation plays a far more pivotal role in environmental conservation.

  • Primarily, environmental challenges such as climate change and air and water pollution do not respect national borders.

    Problems like carbon emissions and ocean plastic pollution can easily spread across countries, so isolated action by individual governments is rarely effective. International agreements enable countries to collectively reduce emissions and establish global environmental standards.

  • Secondly, global cooperation is essential to protect endangered species and natural ecosystems.

    Many rare animals and important habitats, such as rainforests and marine reserves, span multiple countries. Illegal hunting, logging and wildlife trafficking cannot be controlled by one nation alone. Through joint patrols, shared monitoring systems and international bans on illegal wildlife products, countries can work together to prevent species extinction and maintain ecological balance.

总结：
  • In conclusion, while international cooperation boosts global business and technological progress, its role in protecting the environment and preserving ecosystems is irreplaceable. Therefore, the most important benefit of international cooperation should be environmental protection.

13）全球说一种新语言

题目：

Some people say it is necessary to create a new language that improves international communication. Do you think the benefits outweigh the drawbacks?

观点：
  • Some people claim that it is necessary to create a new language to enhance global communication.
  • Although it has certain merits in specific aspects, I firmly believe that its negative impacts far outweigh the positive ones.
问题一：
  • 总起句：Admittedly, to some extent, creating a new universal language can promote international cooperation and bring convenience to people’s daily lives.
  • For one thing, cross-border cooperation between countries can be significantly improved, which will generate more business opportunities.
  • For another, language barriers would no longer exist, allowing people to fully experience and appreciate other countries’ cultural heritage.
问题二：
  • 总起句：However, the popularization of a new universal language will bring more severe negative consequences for both individuals and global cultural diversity.
  • Firstly, the status of native tongues will be diminished, and the traditional culture of each country will be seriously threatened.
  • Besides, individuals engaged in language-related professions will lose their jobs, which will push up the global unemployment rate.
总结：
  • In conclusion, although a newly created universal language can bring some advantages, such as promoting international cooperation and facilitating cross-border business and tourism, its negative impacts are more prominent. It not only threatens the survival of native languages and traditional cultures but also increases the unemployment rate in language-related fields. Therefore, the drawbacks of creating a new global language far outweigh its benefits.

14）年轻人任职政府岗位适配性（背结构）

题目：

Some people think that younger people are not suitable for important positions in government. Others think that it will be a good idea for younger people to take on these positions. Discuss both these views and give your own opinion.

观点：
  • Some people claim that younger generations are not suitable for important government positions, while others hold the opposite view.
  • Although allowing young people to take such crucial roles has certain benefits, I firmly believe the drawbacks far outweigh the advantages.
问题一：
  • 总起句：Admittedly, young people can bring more energy and innovation to governance.
  • They are more creative and better at keeping up with technological trends, thanks to their university education, which enables them to introduce more practical policies.
  • In addition, young people are more energetic and able to cope with heavy workloads, which fits the high-pressure government work. Many are also skilled at digital tools, improving their work efficiency.
问题二：
  • 总起句：Despite these merits, government work requires stability, patience and experience, so young people are not well-suited for it.
  • Firstly, compared with senior politicians, young people lack sufficient experience. They tend to be more impulsive and may jump to conclusions when handling disputes in government affairs, which could harm administrative efficiency or diplomatic relations.
  • Secondly, young people have less public trust and a weak reputation. Gaining public respect usually takes a lifetime, but young politicians often face stereotypes, making it hard for them to win trust.
总结：
  • In conclusion, although young people have strengths in energy and innovation, I believe they should not be the priority for important government positions, as they lack essential experience and public credibility.
